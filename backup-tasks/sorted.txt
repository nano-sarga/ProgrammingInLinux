
Absolute value - The distance of a number from zero on the number line.
Addition - The process of combining two or more numbers to get their total.
Algebra - A branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules for manipulating them.
Binary - A number system using only two digits: 0 and 1.
Calculus - The study of change, through derivatives and integrals.
Combinatorics - The branch of mathematics dealing with counting and arrangements.
Derivative - A measure of how a function changes as its input changes.
Equation - A statement that asserts the equality of two expressions.
Exponent - The number that indicates how many times a base is multiplied by itself.
Factor - A number that divides another number exactly.
Factorial - The product of all positive integers up to a given number, denoted n!.
Function - A relation between a set of inputs and a set of permissible outputs.
Graph - A collection of nodes (vertices) and edges connecting some pairs of nodes.
Greatest common divisor - The largest number that divides two or more integers without a remainder.
Hypotenuse - The side opposite the right angle in a right triangle.
Inequality - A relation expressing that one quantity is greater than or less than another.
Integration - The process of finding the integral of a function.
Intersection - The set containing elements common to two or more sets.
Logarithm - The inverse operation to exponentiation.
Matrix - A rectangular array of numbers arranged in rows and columns.
Number theory - The study of integers and their properties.
Permutation - An arrangement of objects in a specific order.
Prime number - A natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself.
Probability distribution - A function that describes the likelihood of outcomes in an experiment.
Radian - The standard unit of angular measure in mathematics.
Sequence - An ordered list of numbers or objects.
Set - A collection of distinct objects considered as a whole.
Set theory - The branch of mathematical logic that studies sets, or collections of objects.
Statistics - The science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data.
Trigonometry - The study of relationships between angles and sides of triangles.
Union - The set containing all elements from two or more sets.
Vector - A quantity having both magnitude and direction.
